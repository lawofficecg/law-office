Frequently asked questions

Answers to common questions about your rights, our process, and what to expect.

California law requires landlords to keep rental properties in a livable condition. This includes maintaining working heat, plumbing, and electrical systems, as well as keeping the property free of mold, pests, and other health hazards. When a landlord fails to meet these standards, tenants have legal rights.
Start by sending written notice to your landlord documenting the problem and requesting repairs. Keep copies of everything. If they fail to respond or make repairs, you may have a legal claim. We recommend getting an air and mold test done to document the conditions.
Generally, you may have a case if someone else's negligence caused your injury. The best way to find out is to speak with an attorney. We offer free consultations and will give you an honest assessment.
Mold and habitability cases are handled on a contingency-fee basis — you pay no attorney fees unless your case resolves in your favor. There is no upfront cost and no fee if there is no recovery. If your case settles or results in a judgment, the attorney fee is calculated as a percentage of that recovery.
Personal injury fee arrangements vary by case. Please contact us to discuss the specifics of your situation. We offer free consultations and are happy to explain how fees work before you decide anything.
Every case is different. Some settle in a few months, others take longer. We'll give you a realistic timeline once we understand your situation.
We conduct consultations in English, but a Spanish-language interpreter can be arranged upon request.
We serve clients throughout Southern California.
