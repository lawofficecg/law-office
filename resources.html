Tips for Tenants

How to Document Habitability Issues

If you're experiencing unsafe conditions in your rental, documenting everything is one of the most important things you can do. Here are some practical steps:

  • Take dated photos and videos of the problem (mold, leaks, pests, etc.)
  • Send written notice to your landlord by email or certified mail — keep copies
  • Keep a log of when you reported the issue and what the response was
  • Get a professional mold or air quality test done if you suspect contamination
  • Seek medical attention if you're experiencing health symptoms
  • Save all communication with your landlord or property management
Personal Injury

Common Mistakes After an Injury

After an injury, small decisions can affect how a claim is evaluated. Preserving evidence early can make a significant difference.

  • Leaving without reporting the incident
  • Failing to photograph the dangerous condition
  • Not getting witness names
  • Waiting too long to seek medical treatment
  • Throwing away shoes, clothing, or damaged items
  • Giving a recorded statement before understanding the claim
  • Signing broad medical authorizations too early
  • Posting about the incident, injuries, or case online
  • Ignoring follow-up medical care
  • Waiting until video footage or records disappear
Personal Injury

Personal Injury Case Checklist

If you were injured because of a dangerous property condition, unsafe walkway, fall, or other incident, the information below can help our office evaluate your potential claim.

  • Date and location of the incident
  • What caused the injury
  • Who owned, managed, or controlled the property
  • Whether the incident was reported
  • Names and contact information for witnesses
  • Photos or videos of the hazard
  • Medical treatment received
  • Injuries, symptoms, and ongoing limitations
  • Medical bills, lost wages, or missed work
  • Any insurance communications
