Privacy Policy

Last Updated: June 30, 2026

What Information We Collect

When you submit our intake form, we collect the following information that you voluntarily provide:

  • Full name
  • Phone number
  • Email address
  • Best time to reach you
  • Case type (habitability or personal injury)
  • Written notice status (whether you have notified your landlord in writing)
  • Landlord response
  • Air test or mold test results
  • Symptoms you are experiencing
  • Description of your situation
  • Incident date (for personal injury matters)
  • Medical treatment information

How We Use Your Information

We use the information you provide solely for the following purposes:

  • Evaluating your potential case to determine whether we may be able to assist you
  • Contacting you in response to your inquiry

No Attorney-Client Relationship

Submitting the intake form does not create an attorney-client relationship. An attorney-client relationship is only established when you have been formally retained in writing by the Law Office of Caroline Golian.

We Do Not Sell Your Information

We do not sell, rent, or share your personal information with third parties for marketing or advertising purposes.

Cookies and Tracking

This website uses browser local storage to remember your cookie consent preference so you are not asked again on each visit. No tracking or advertising cookies are set without your explicit consent.

Analytics scripts and third-party tracking tools are only loaded if you click "Accept" on the cookie consent banner. If you click "Reject," no tracking scripts are loaded at all. You can change your choice at any time using the "Cookie Preferences" link in the footer.

This website also loads fonts from Google's servers (fonts.googleapis.com and fonts.gstatic.com). When your browser requests these fonts, Google may log that request in accordance with Google's Privacy Policy. No additional data from this website is shared with Google.

Data Security

We take reasonable steps to protect the information you submit. However, no method of transmission over the Internet is completely secure, and we cannot guarantee absolute security.
